CSRA Health Matters

The Use Case Community for this Care Unit is in Augusta-Richmond[1] (“Augusta”), Georgia. Located on the north east border of the state, adjacent to South Carolina, Augusta is the second oldest city in Georgia with a population slightly greater than 200,000 people. Fifty-eight percent of thepopulation is between the ages of 20 and 64 and another 31% of the population is less than 19 years of age. The population is relatively homogenous in that 52% of the population is African American and 44% is Caucasian, with only 4% of the population representing other races. According to the most recent census, there are 74,618 households in the metropolitan area with a mean income of $68,000, yet almost 31,000 households report an income of less than $29,999 annually; representing 41% of the households in the community.

Health Profile for Augusta-Richmond

The population of Georgia has higher rates of diabetes, obesity and hypertension than the rest of the nation. Georgia experiences the second highest death rate/100,000 due to hypertension in the United States at an average of 11.51, compared to the national average of 7.38 deaths per 100,000. This is second only to the state of Mississippi. Augusta-Richmond County, where this Use Case Community is located, reports 15.7 deaths per 100,000.
